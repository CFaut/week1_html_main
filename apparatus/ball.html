Ball:

Ball is by far one of the hardest pieces of equipment to us in Rhythmic gymnastics.

It may make for beautiful routines but if it falls or drops in any way - it rolls. This causes lots of deductions and can cost you the competition.

Routines often include rolling the ball on and around the body to display skill as well as intricate throws, turns and balances.

It is a very expressive piece of apparatus and gymnasts often use more sad/emotional music for their routines.

  • The ball is usually made of soft plastic or rubber.
  • It must be 18-20cm in diameter and be at least 400g.
  • The ball may not be grasped and has to be held gently, otherwise points are deducted.
  • Can be any color - and may have glitter or shine to its appearence.
