What is Rhythmic Gymnastics?
  • Rhythmic gymnastics is a sport done with 5 different types of appararus that combines dancing, gymnastics and calisthenics.
  • It was first recognised as a sport in 1963 by the International Gymnastics Federation and in 1984 it officialy became an Olympic sport.
  • Gymnasts who compete in the Olympics usually start training between the gaes of 3-5 years old and reach their peak performance between the ages of 15 and 19.
  • Gymnasts can also compete individually or in group events consisting of 5 gymnasts.
  • A typical routine is about 1 minute 30 seconds long and gymnasts are judged on their artistry, execution and difficulty skills.
