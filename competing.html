The Scoring System

Code of Points

The final score is the sum of the difficulty, execution and srtistry scores.

  1. Difficulty:

    • Consists of body difficulties such as jumps, balances and turns.
    • Includes risks such as dynamic elements with rotation and apparatus difficulties
    • Evaluated during the routine with a predetermined difficulty sheet.

  2. Execution:

    • The degree of perfection in the routine.
    • This includes the technical handling of the apparatus and straight arms, legs and pointed toes in certain movements
    • Starts with a value of 10, and points are deducted when there are mistakes.

  3. Artistry:

    • Refers to the overall presentation and artistic element of the routine
    • Does the routine and movements fit in with the rhythem of the music?
    • Is the gymnast expessive? Do they bring atmosphere?
    • Also looks at finer movements of the hands and body.
